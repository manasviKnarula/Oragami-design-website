Facts About Camels:
  • Camels don’t just spit for fun. If they feel threatened, they will use it as a defense mechanism.
  • The word ‘camel’ comes from an Arabic word, when it’s literally translated, it means beauty.
  • During the Second World War, German tank drivers would drive their vehicles over camel droppings, thinking it would bring them good luck.
  • In Arabian culture, they are so iconic that there are more than 160 words which mean ‘camel’.
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • camels also communicate with members of their group by making sounds like moans and bellows.


Facts About chameleon:
  • When a Chameleon is first born they are called hatchlings.
  • On average, a chameleon's tongue is roughly twice the length of its body. In humans, that would be a tongue about 10 to 12 feet (about 3 to 4 meters) long.
  • They can detect sound frequencies in the range of 200–600 Hz.
  • chameleons can see the same colors we do plus ultraviolet light, which we cannot see.
  • Chameleons in distress turn very dark and dull-colored, so that's typically what color they are when dead.
  • Happy and relaxed chameleons will be bright green and blue at home.


Facts About cicada:
  • Cicadas are super loud. A bunch of male cicadas can get really loud!
  • Cicadas live a really long time. A periodical cicada nymph climbs out of the ground after 17 years of living off tree roots
  • Cicadas shed their skin.
  • Cicadas get easily confused
  • Cicadas lay a lot of eggs
  • You can eat cicadas


Facts About panda:
  • They have great camouflage for their environment
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • Cubs are well protected in their first month.
  • Bamboo is critical to their diet.
  • At 5 months old, giant pandas learn how to climb - sometimes practicing by climbing on their mum.
  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.


Facts About pigeon:
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  • Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours.
  • Fastest known pigeon managed to reach speed on 92 miles per hour.
